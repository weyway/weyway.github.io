The new Frontline Mode

In this new map, there will be slight differences in how it plays out.

Going on the offensive and taking¬†Triumphs will be your keys to victory.

The Triumphs cannot be retaken by others once they're taken¬†(where have we seen this before) but this time tho, there will be weather effects that will happen across the battlefield, will these ruin you, or give you a chance to turn the fight around?

Claiming a Triumph

Remember those sneaky players skipping the group fights and going out to take out nodes without anyone noticing? Well, it seems this system will not allow for that kind of tactic.
This will be a system built around pushing objectives with superior might at the helm, forcing the enemy to give you the space necessary to capture the points.

From what we're led to believe, the new capture system will need you to capture three mini circles in order to claim the node.
Having more than 1 player in a circle won't make any difference, and to claim the node, you need to plant your team's flag in each of the circles.
For each shape of node, there will be a specific amount of circles¬†(one for each corner),¬†for example, a triangle node will have 3 flag circles, a pentagonal node will have 5.

If a player from an opposing team stands on a circle with you, you won't be able to claim it, neither will it work if they don't let you plant all the flags.

Same with players entering or leaving the circles, the capture will start from zero so you can't leave the circle at all while the capture is taking place.

This system will focus more on fighting and less on interacting with nodes and waiting for a cast bar.

Systems and Tech

That covers all the content, now we're looking into the updates to the game itself, there's a couple different things, let's go!

Glamour Update

The recap for this one is kinda short.

You will be able to use any gear in your glamours, irrespective of class, job or level restrictions!

There will only be two limitations, these being;

  1. weapons and tools are only able to be used by the corresponding class due to animations (e.g. two-handed swords can only be glamoured into other two-handed sword);
  2. and racial gear, which will continue to be limited to races.

In response to the whole blacklist issues that arised in the past, they made some changes to the blacklist system to further obscure data that might be used to determine who's characters belong to which player.

Some images of Yoshi-P showcasing the changes.

Outfit Glamour Update

Some quality of life updates to the Outfit system.

  • Certain class/job exclusive gear, starting gear and "fashion gear" (other than lv.1) will now be treated as outfit glamour sets
  • Individual items that are outfit enabled can now be converted without requiring the complete set (e.g. if you have just 1 or 2 pieces of a set, you can store it as an outfit and add the rest later)
  • When storing new glams, it will detect automatically if there's a piece already stored that it can combine with and complete that set.

Command Panel

This one was kept mostly in the shadows until the last live letter, and it's now ready and coming on 7.4, woah!

For those that use arcane art stuffs, this is very much like a simplified TPie, except box shaped instead of circle shaped!

What is it, you ask?

  • A new UI element where anything that can be placed on a normal hotbar can be allocated to.
  • This will provide you with a "shortcut box" that has¬†4 tabs and 25 slots on each tab
  • This "box" can be shown and hidden whenever you want and is necessary (e.g. you can store all your favourite emotes inside box-chan and save space on your hotbars)
  • There will be some adjustable settings such as leaving it open as an additional hotbar or having it open at cursor position, close on action, etc.

What this system will be most useful for is storing those things that are not regularly used, such as job gear macros, emotes, your favourite mounts, etc.

Here's some images of this in-game.

Strategy Board

This one has been long awaited.

You can finally create and display strategy diagrams throughout a duty, with the possibility to share your strategies and display the same board across the entire party, even allowing party members to edit it in real time!

These strategy boards will have to be entirely created by the community, but some presets will be available as well to help you get started.
You can also receive a strategy board directly from a party member, or even "install" them via codes that can be shared by players online.

The system will have a storage limit of 50 strategies¬†total, which can be contained in¬†folders with up to 10 strategies within.

They have even considered differences in how regions display their strategies, allowing you to change the display settings of the strategy board depending on how it makes sense to you, allowing you to highlight your own role, etc.

The boards can display mechanics as easily or as complex as you wish them to, even featuring layers to help manage the complexity and visibility of things (e.g. place job markers on a higher layer than the AoE markers).

The "screen sharing" mode which allows you to edit the board in real time for whole party strategy lessons might be a tiny bit laggy, but this is due to them routing the traffic of the boards through the server and it's the best that could be done considering the limitations.
